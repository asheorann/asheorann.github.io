2. Serif Typeface Options

Each shown at 18px with 1.65 line-height (the body text size)

Option A: Source Serif 4

What does it mean to hold a belief lightly? I've been thinking about epistemic humility—not as a performance of uncertainty, but as a genuine stance toward knowledge. The difference between "I don't know" as a deflection and "I don't know" as an invitation to think together. There's something about the texture of good thinking that resists certainty without collapsing into relativism.

Character: Clean, contemporary, designed for screen. Feels like modern editorial—Stripe Press territory. Not precious, not academic. Good readability, doesn't call attention to itself.

Option B: Newsreader

What does it mean to hold a belief lightly? I've been thinking about epistemic humility—not as a performance of uncertainty, but as a genuine stance toward knowledge. The difference between "I don't know" as a deflection and "I don't know" as an invitation to think together. There's something about the texture of good thinking that resists certainty without collapsing into relativism.

Character: More contrast than Source Serif, slightly narrower. Feels like quality journalism or a well-set magazine. More classical but still very readable. Good middle ground.

Option C: EB Garamond

What does it mean to hold a belief lightly? I've been thinking about epistemic humility—not as a performance of uncertainty, but as a genuine stance toward knowledge. The difference between "I don't know" as a deflection and "I don't know" as an invitation to think together. There's something about the texture of good thinking that resists certainty without collapsing into relativism.

Character: The most romantic choice. Beautiful, classical, high contrast. Book energy—essays that feel like they could be printed and bound. Most opinionated. Risk: can feel affected if the writing doesn't match the typography's weight.
