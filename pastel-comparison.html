Less Common Fonts for Certain Elements

Keeping Source Serif 4 for body text, but these could be used for headings, hero, or special sections

Crimson Pro — for headings or hero
Anushka Orekondy

A Google Font with old-style numerals and classical proportions, but designed for screen. Less common than Garamond but with similar elegance. Could work beautifully for your name / hero section while keeping Source Serif for body text.

Literata — for headings or hero
Anushka Orekondy

Originally commissioned for Google Play Books. Variable font with lots of personality. Slightly narrow, good contrast, feels literary without being stuffy. Would make distinctive headings while Source Serif handles body prose.

Spectral — for headings or body
Anushka Orekondy

Designed for Google Docs but rarely seen in personal sites. Open, friendly, highly legible. If you wanted something more distinctive than Source Serif but still very readable for body text, this is a strong candidate. Or just use it for headings.

My Recommendation for Mix

Accent: Pastel Marigold (#E8B563) or Terracotta Clay (#C9976B)
Body text: Source Serif 4 (as you chose)
Headings + nav: Inter (stays clean and functional)
Hero name: Crimson Pro or Literata (adds distinction)
Metadata/labels: JetBrains Mono (as planned)

This gives you the naturey, grounded pastel feel while keeping readability high. The distinctive serif for your name creates a signature moment without overwhelming the whole site.
